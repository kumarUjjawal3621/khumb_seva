The Divine Story

What is Simhastha Kumbh Mela Nashik?

The Nashik–Trimbakeshwar Kumbh Mela, also known as Simhastha, is one of the four sacred Kumbh Melas held in India — alongside Prayagraj, Haridwar, and Ujjain. It stands apart as the only Kumbh governed by the planet Guru (Jupiter) entering the zodiac sign of Simha (Leo), a celestial event that occurs once every twelve years.


Unlike any other Kumbh, the Nashik Simhastha is a Dual Kumbh — the divine energy flows across two distinct sacred power centers separated by 30 km: Ramkund in Panchavati for the Vaishnava Akhadas, and Kushavarta in Trimbakeshwar for the Shaiva Akhadas. This sacred duality makes it uniquely profound among all the Kumbh gatherings.


During this Parva, the waters of Goda-Mai — the Godavari River, revered as the Ganges of the South — are believed to be charged with extraordinary cosmic energy, transmuted into Amrit, the nectar of immortality. A single holy dip (Shahi Snan) during the prescribed auspicious moments is said to cleanse the karma of countless lifetimes and open the gates of Moksha.

Sacred River

The Godavari — "Goda-Mai" — originates in the Brahmagiri hills of Trimbakeshwar and is revered as the Dakshina Ganga (Ganges of the South), one of India's most holy rivers.

The Amrit Legend

During the cosmic churning of the ocean (Samudra Manthan), drops of Amrit (immortal nectar) fell at four places on Earth — Nashik being one of them — sanctifying it for eternity.

13 Akhadas

All thirteen major Akhadas — Shaiva, Vaishnava, and Udasin — converge at Nashik-Trimbakeshwar, bringing together thousands of Sadhus, Naga Babas, Mahamandaleshwars, and Acharyas.

Panchavati Connection

Nashik holds the sacred distinction of being the site of the Panchavati forest, where Lord Ram, Sita Mata, and Lakshman resided during their exile — making it a centre of both Shaiva and Vaishnava reverence.

The Celestial Science

Trikhandi Yog — त्रिखंडी योग

The Trikhandi Yog is the rarest and most sacred astronomical conjunction that governs the Nashik Simhastha Kumbh — a celestial alignment so powerful that the ancient sages designated it as the supreme moment of liberation on earth.

The name Trikhandi (तीन खंड — three celestial segments or realms) refers to the simultaneous alignment of three cosmic forces across the three realms of existence: Swarga (the heavens), Martya (the earth), and Patal (the underworld). When these three realms align through the positions of specific celestial bodies, the veil between the mortal and the divine becomes the thinnest.

This Yoga is activated by three specific planetary conditions occurring simultaneously:

1. Guru in Simha Rashi — Jupiter, the planet of Dharma and wisdom, enters Leo (Simha). This is the primary trigger of the entire Nashik Kumbh. In Vedic astronomy, Jupiter in Leo is considered the most auspicious cosmic position for purification and moksha.

2. Surya in Simha Rashi — The Sun also occupies Leo, amplifying the solar-divine energy. The Sun's presence in its own sign (Leo is the Sun's own house in Jyotisha) creates a Swagrihi Surya — the sovereign Sun in full power.

3. Chandra in specific Nakshatra — The Moon's placement in a designated sacred Nakshatra on the day of Shahi Snan locks the Trikhandi Yog into its full potency, creating the precise Muhurta during which bathing bestows liberation.

According to the Nashik Mahatmya in the Skandha Purana, bathing at Kushavarta or Ramkund during the Trikhandi Yog grants the spiritual merit equivalent to a thousand Ashwamedha Yagnas and ten million pilgrimages to all sacred tirthas combined.

This is not merely folklore — it is a sophisticated system of Vedic astronomy and spiritual science, refined over millennia, that recognizes the earth's sacred geography as a receiver of cosmic transmissions, with the Godavari as its most powerful antenna.

गुरु सिंह राशि

Jupiter in Leo (Simha) — The foundational celestial event that awakens the Kumbh. Occurs once every ~12 years. Jupiter's wisdom energy pours into the Godavari, sanctifying its waters as Amrit.

स्वगृही सूर्य

Sun in Leo (Swagrihi) — The Sun in its own sign amplifies divine solar energy. The Godavari reflects this as Surya's grace, purifying all who bathe in her waters during this period.

चंद्र नक्षत्र

Moon in Sacred Nakshatra — The Moon's position in the designated Nakshatra on each Shahi Snan date forms the final lock of the Trikhandi Yog, creating the precise divine window (Muhurta) for liberation.

त्रिखंडी योग फल

The Fruit of Trikhandi Yog — According to Skandha Purana, a single bath during this Yoga equals the merit of a thousand Ashwamedha Yagnas and liberates the bather and seven generations of ancestors.

The Two Sacred Poles

The Dual Kumbh — Twin Power Centres

Unlike any other Kumbh in India, the Nashik Simhastha is uniquely blessed with two sacred bathing sites, each presided over by its own Akhada tradition, each thundering with a distinct divine name.

🪷

Ramkund — Panchavati, Nashik

रामकुंड, पंचवटी, नाशिक

Presiding Tradition: Vaishnava Akhadas

Sacred Chant: "Jai Shri Ram"


Ramkund is a sacred kund (pool) in the Panchavati forest where Lord Ram is believed to have bathed and offered water to his ancestors during his 14-year exile. The site pulsates with Vaishnava devotion — it is here that the Ram-bhakti tradition merges with the Kumbh's cosmic waters. During Simhastha, the Vaishnava Akhadas perform their Shahi Snan here in colourful processions of elephants, palanquins, and chariots.

🔱

Kushavarta — Trimbakeshwar

कुशावर्त, त्र्यंबकेश्वर

Presiding Tradition: Shaiva Akhadas

Sacred Chant: "Har Har Mahadev"


Kushavarta is the sacred kund at Trimbakeshwar, the very source of the Godavari River emerging from the Brahmagiri hills. Trimbakeshwar also houses one of the twelve sacred Jyotirlingas — Trimbakeshwar Mahadev — making this location among the holiest Shaiva pilgrimages on earth. During Simhastha, the awe-inspiring Naga Babas and Shaiva Akhadas descend in procession, ash-covered and trident-bearing, roaring Har Har Mahadev.

In Three Sacred Tongues

The Call to Pilgrimage

सिंहस्थ कुंभ मेला नाशिक २०२७ में आपका स्वागत है

बारह वर्षों की प्रतीक्षा का अंत होने वाला है। नाशिक-त्र्यंबकेश्वर सिंहस्थ कुंभ मेला केवल एक सामूहिक आयोजन नहीं है — यह एक दुर्लभ खगोलीय संरेखण है जो भारत की आध्यात्मिक नींव को जागृत कर देता है।

जब गुरु ग्रह सिंह राशि में प्रवेश करते हैं, तो पवित्र गोदावरी नदी — दक्षिण की गंगा — के जल में अमृत की शक्ति समाहित हो जाती है। स्कंद पुराण में वर्णित नाशिक महात्म्य के अनुसार, इस पवित्र महापर्व में एक बार स्नान करने से सहस्र अश्वमेध यज्ञों का पुण्य प्राप्त होता है और सात पीढ़ियों के पूर्वजों का उद्धार होता है।

महत्वपूर्ण स्नान तिथियाँ:

🪷 प्रथम शाही स्नान — २ अगस्त २०२७, आषाढ सोमवती अमावस्या
🪷 द्वितीय शाही स्नान — ३१ अगस्त २०२७, श्रावण अमावस्या
🪷 तृतीय शाही स्नान — ११ सितंबर २०२७ (नाशिक) · १२ सितंबर २०२७ (त्र्यंबकेश्वर)

त्रिखंडी योग — तीन लोकों (स्वर्ग, मर्त्य, पाताल) का एक साथ संरेखण — इस कुंभ को अन्य सभी कुंभों से श्रेष्ठ और अद्वितीय बनाता है। पंचवटी के रामकुंड में वैष्णव अखाड़े और त्र्यंबकेश्वर के कुशावर्त में शैव अखाड़े — दोनों शाही स्नानों की पवित्र परंपरा इस दिव्य संगम को अविस्मरणीय बनाती है।

आइए, इस महाकुंभ में सम्मिलित हों — जहाँ जल केवल जल नहीं, बल्कि मोक्ष का द्वार है।

॥ हर हर गंगे · जय गोदावरी माँ · हर हर महादेव ॥

सिंहस्थ कुंभमेळा नाशिक २०२७ मध्ये आपले स्वागत आहे

बारा वर्षांची प्रतीक्षा संपत आली आहे. नाशिक-त्र्यंबकेश्वर सिंहस्थ कुंभमेळा हा केवळ एक जत्रा नाही — तो एक विश्वनाट्य आहे, ज्यात आकाश, पृथ्वी आणि गोदावरी माता एकत्र येऊन मोक्षाचे दार उघडतात.

गुरु ग्रह सिंह राशीत प्रवेश करतो तेव्हा, गोदावरीच्या पाण्यात अमृताची शक्ती संचारते असे स्कंद पुराणातील नाशिक माहात्म्य सांगते. या महापर्वावर एकदा स्नान केल्याने हजार अश्वमेध यज्ञांचे पुण्य मिळते आणि सात पिढ्यांचा उद्धार होतो, असा आपल्या पूर्वजांचा विश्वास आहे.

महत्त्वाच्या स्नान तिथी:

🪷 प्रथम शाही स्नान — २ ऑगस्ट २०२७, आषाढ सोमवती अमावस्या
🪷 द्वितीय शाही स्नान — ३१ ऑगस्ट २०२७, श्रावण अमावस्या
🪷 तृतीय शाही स्नान — ११ सप्टेंबर २०२७ (नाशिक) · १२ सप्टेंबर २०२७ (त्र्यंबकेश्वर)

त्रिखंडी योग म्हणजे तीन लोकांचा — स्वर्ग, मर्त्य आणि पाताल — एकत्रित संयोग. हा योग नाशिकच्या सिंहस्थाला इतर कुंभांपेक्षा वेगळा आणि अतिशय श्रेष्ठ बनवतो. पंचवटीतील रामकुंडावर वैष्णव अखाड्यांचा शाही स्नान सोहळा "जय श्रीराम"च्या जयघोषात होतो, तर त्र्यंबकेश्वरातील कुशावर्तावर शैव अखाड्यांचा सोहळा "हर हर महादेव"च्या गर्जनेत संपन्न होतो.

नाशिक — ज्या भूमीवर प्रभू श्रीराम, सीतामाता आणि लक्ष्मण यांनी वनवास काळात निवास केला, जेथे त्र्यंबकेश्वर ज्योतिर्लिंग आहे, जिथून गोदावरी माता उगम पावते — त्या पवित्र भूमीवर आपले स्वागत आहे.

॥ हर हर गंगे · गोदावरी माऊलीचा विजय असो · हर हर महादेव ॥
